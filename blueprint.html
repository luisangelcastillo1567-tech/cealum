MODULE 02 OF 04
Finding Clients — The Google Maps Method

You do not need a following. You do not need ads. You need a phone and Google Maps. Local businesses — restaurants, salons, contractors, real estate agents, gyms, law offices — are actively losing money every day because they lack basic digital systems. You are going to fix that for them.

The Prospecting Process

2.1Open Google Maps. Search any business type in your city (e.g. "plumbers Las Vegas"). Filter to businesses with under 50 reviews. These are small operations — exactly your target.
2.2Check each business: do they have a website? Is it mobile-friendly? Do they have a Google Business profile? Missing any of these = your opening.
2.3Write down: business name, phone number, what's missing. Build a list of 20 per day. That's your pipeline.
2.4Call them. Use the script below. Your goal on the first call is not to sell — it's to schedule a 15-minute discovery call.

Cold Call Script — First Contact

Word for word — memorize this

"Hi, is this [Owner Name]? My name is [Your Name] — I'm a digital consultant based here in [City]. I was doing some research on local [business type] businesses and I noticed your [website is outdated / you don't have a website / your Google listing is incomplete]. I help businesses like yours fix that really quickly — usually within a week. I'm not trying to sell you anything on this call, I just wanted to see if it'd be worth a 15-minute conversation. Are you free sometime this week?"

If they ask "how much does it cost?"

"It really depends on what you need — that's exactly why I'd love the 15 minutes. Some clients pay $300, some pay $1,500. But I won't know until I understand your situation. Can we just get 15 minutes on the calendar first?"

If they say "I'm not interested"

"Totally understand. Can I ask — do you currently have a way to follow up with customers automatically after they visit? No pressure, I'm just curious." (This often opens the door to a real conversation.)

Protecting Yourself

4.1Always collect 50% deposit before starting any work. Non-negotiable. Use PayPal Invoices or Stripe.
4.2Use a simple 1-page contract. At minimum cover: scope of work, payment terms, revision limits (max 2 rounds), and ownership rights.
